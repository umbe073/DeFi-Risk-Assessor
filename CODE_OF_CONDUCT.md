# Hodler Suite UAB Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

Hodler Suite UAB expects everyone working in or around this repository to act
professionally, respectfully, and lawfully. This applies to employees,
contractors, vendors, approved partners, and any other authorized contributor.

This code exists for a private SaaS company environment. It does not make this
repository public, open source, or open for unsolicited contribution.

## Our Standards

Expected behavior includes:

* Demonstrating empathy and kindness toward other people
* Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
* Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback
* Accepting responsibility and apologizing to those affected by our mistakes,
  and learning from the experience
* Protecting Hodler Suite UAB confidential information, customer data,
  credentials, operational details, and intellectual property
* Following security, privacy, deployment, and incident-response processes

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

* The use of sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or
  advances of any kind
* Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
* Public or private harassment
* Publishing others' private information, such as a physical or email
  address, without their explicit permission
* Disclosing Hodler Suite UAB confidential information outside approved systems
* Mishandling customer data, security reports, credentials, logs, or production
  access
* Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a
  professional setting

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Hodler Suite UAB leadership and authorized project maintainers are responsible
for clarifying and enforcing acceptable behavior.

Project maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject
comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are
not aligned to this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for moderation
decisions when appropriate.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in repository activity, issues, pull requests, CI
logs, Slack channels, support workflows, incidents, deployments, meetings, and
other company-approved workspaces.

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be
reported to Hodler Suite UAB leadership or the security/compliance contact at
[compliance@hodler-suite.com](mailto:compliance@hodler-suite.com).
All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

All reviewers are expected to respect the privacy and security of the
reporter of any incident.

## Enforcement Guidelines

Hodler Suite UAB will use these impact guidelines when determining
the consequences for any action they deem in violation of this Code of Conduct:

### 1. Correction

**Company Impact**: Use of inappropriate language or other behavior deemed
unprofessional or unwelcome in the workplace or repository.

**Consequence**: A private, written warning from Hodler Suite UAB leadership or
project maintainers, providing
clarity around the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the
behavior was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

### 2. Warning

**Company Impact**: A violation through a single incident or series
of actions.

**Consequence**: A warning with consequences for continued behavior. No
interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with
those enforcing the Code of Conduct, for a specified period of time. Violating
these terms may lead to suspension of repository or system access.

### 3. Temporary Ban

**Company Impact**: A serious violation of company standards, including
sustained inappropriate behavior.

**Consequence**: Temporary suspension of repository, Slack, system, or vendor
access for a specified period of time. No interaction with the people involved,
including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, is
allowed during this period. Violating these terms may lead to permanent removal
of access.

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Company Impact**: Demonstrating a pattern of violation of company
standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior,  harassment of an
individual, or aggression toward or disparagement of classes of individuals.

**Consequence**: Permanent removal from repository access and related company
workspaces, subject to applicable agreements and company process.
