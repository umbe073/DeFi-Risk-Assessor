Privacy Policy

Effective date: March 1, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how Hodler Suite collects, uses, stores, and protects personal data and technical data processed through the platform, including website access, account management, support tickets, and billing-related workflows.

1. Data Controller Context

Hodler Suite acts as the service operator for platform accounts and core operational records. Depending on implementation and enterprise agreements, customers may act as independent controllers for data they submit through the Service.

2. Data Categories We Process

  • Account data: email address, user ID, role, authentication metadata, security reset flows.
  • Security data: password hashes, 2FA/TOTP metadata, verification code events, anti-abuse/rate-limit signals.
  • Support data: ticket subject/body, status transitions, conversation history, operational notifications.
  • Billing data: plan identifiers, checkout sessions, order references, payment-state callbacks, subscription state.
  • Technical telemetry: access logs, service status samples, latency/error metrics used for reliability and security operations.

3. Purpose of Processing

  • Provide account access, authentication, and role-based authorization.
  • Operate support and communication workflows.
  • Process subscription lifecycle events and payment-state reconciliation.
  • Maintain platform security, fraud prevention, abuse mitigation, and incident response.
  • Improve reliability, performance, and operational observability.

3A. Automated Processing Notice

Risk scores, anomaly tags, and red-flag indicators are generated through automated analytics. These outputs support operational decisioning but are not fully autonomous legal determinations. Human review is recommended for material financial/compliance decisions.

4. Legal Bases (General)

  • Contractual necessity: account and subscription operations.
  • Legitimate interests: service security, fraud prevention, diagnostics, and reliability controls.
  • Compliance obligations: where legally required for records, law enforcement cooperation, or regulatory compliance.
  • Consent: where specific optional processing requires it under applicable law.

5. Data Sources

Data may be collected directly from users and indirectly via integrated processors/providers (for example payment, email, anti-bot, and external API services).

6. Recipients and Processors

We may share data with vetted processors strictly for service delivery (such as payment gateways, email delivery/receiving providers, infrastructure providers, and traffic protection services). We do not sell personal data.

7. International Transfers

Depending on provider regions and customer location, data may be processed in multiple jurisdictions. Where required, we apply contractual and technical safeguards appropriate to transfer risk.

8. Retention

  • Account and subscription records are retained while accounts are active and for a reasonable period thereafter for legal/security continuity.
  • Support conversations and operational event logs are retained for troubleshooting, accountability, and service improvement.
  • Security and anti-abuse telemetry retention is scoped to operational necessity and legal obligations.

8A. Data Minimization and Redaction

Public documentation, external knowledge-base materials, and non-production artifacts are processed through redaction controls to remove secret tokens, sensitive infrastructure paths, and private-origin network details before publication.

9. Security Controls

  • Password hashing, session controls, role-based access boundaries, and optional 2FA.
  • CSRF protections, security headers, reverse-proxy controls, and anti-bot challenge flow.
  • Operational monitoring, service-status probes, and reconciliation workflows for critical billing/support pathways.

No system can guarantee absolute security; however, we implement layered controls designed for practical risk reduction and rapid incident response.

10. User Rights

Subject to applicable law, users may request access, correction, deletion, restriction, portability, or objection regarding personal data processing. We may require identity verification and may decline requests where legal exceptions apply.

10A. Contact for Privacy Requests

For access/correction/deletion requests, submit a support ticket from the platform Help Center and include your account email plus User ID so requests can be authenticated and processed securely.

11. Cookies and Similar Technologies

The platform uses essential session/security mechanisms and may use technical controls (including challenge verification) to prevent abuse and preserve integrity. Optional analytics/tracking should be configured in accordance with your jurisdictional requirements.

12. Children’s Data

The Service is not directed to children. If you believe a child has provided personal data in violation of applicable law, contact support for review and remediation.

13. Policy Updates

We may update this Privacy Policy when required by legal, operational, or product changes. Updated versions are effective upon publication, with an updated effective date.

For usage rules, disclaimers, and platform obligations, see Terms of Use.
